Educational Tours

Day 1: The Jaisalmer Fort is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and offers stunning views of the city. After exploring the fort, students can visit the Patwon Ki Haveli, a group of five havelis built by a wealthy merchant in the 19th century. In the evening, students can take a desert safari and explore the stunning sand dunes. They can enjoy camel rides and witness a beautiful sunset over the desert. Camping in the desert is also an option for those who want to experience the full beauty of the Thar Desert.

Day 2: The Bada Bagh is a garden complex that houses cenotaphs of the former rulers of Jaisalmer. The Salim Singh Ki Haveli is an 18th-century haveli that offers a glimpse of the city's cultural heritage and architecture. The Gadisar Lake is a 14th-century man-made lake that offers a serene environment and is a great place to relax after a busy day of sightseeing.

Day 3: Start your day with a visit to the Jain Temples, which are a group of temples that were built in the 12th and 15th centuries. The temples are known for their intricate carvings and stunning architecture. The Jain Temples are known for their intricate carvings and stunning architecture, while the Nathmal Ki Haveli offers a glimpse of the city's rich cultural heritage and is a great way to learn about the local way of life. The sand dunes offer a unique and unforgettable experience.

This 3-day itinerary is perfect for student college groups who want to explore Jaisalmer's stunning landscapes and learn about its rich cultural heritage
